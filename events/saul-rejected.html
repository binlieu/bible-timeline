Summary

Saul disobeys the command concerning Amalek and tries to justify his actions to Samuel. Samuel announces that the Lord has rejected Saul from continuing as king.

Saul defeats Amalek but spares Agag and keeps the best livestock, directly disobeying the command Samuel delivered. When confronted, Saul frames the spoil as material for sacrifice, but Samuel declares that obedience is better than sacrifice.

The rejection does not immediately remove Saul from the throne, but it marks the decisive theological break in his reign. From this point the narrative looks for a king whose rule will be shaped by covenant obedience rather than selective compliance.

Historical Background

Historical reconstruction: The Amalekite conflict reflects Israel's memory of long hostility with desert raiding groups. Scholarly opinion: The passage is primarily theological history, explaining why Saul's dynasty did not continue.
