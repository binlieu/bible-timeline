Summary

Jeremiah ministers from Josiah's reign through Jerusalem's destruction. He warns Judah to surrender to Babylonian judgment while also promising a new covenant and future restoration.

Jeremiah's ministry spans the final decades of Judah, from reform hopes under Josiah to crisis under Babylon. He confronts false confidence in the temple, calls for repentance, and suffers rejection for announcing judgment.

The duration of his ministry lets the book document the slow collapse of Judah's political and spiritual life. Yet Jeremiah also promises that God will write his law on the heart and restore his people after exile.

Historical Background

Historical reconstruction: Jeremiah's career overlaps the rise of Babylon after Assyria's decline and Egypt's failed intervention. Archaeological evidence: Bullae from late monarchic Judah preserve names also known from Jeremiah, though not every identification is certain.
