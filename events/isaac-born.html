Summary

Isaac is born to Abraham and Sarah as the promised child through whom the covenant line continues.

God keeps his promise to Abraham and Sarah by giving them a son in their old age. Isaac's birth demonstrates that the covenant family exists because of God's faithfulness, not human strength.

The name Isaac, connected with laughter, recalls both the seeming impossibility of the promise and the joy of its fulfillment.
