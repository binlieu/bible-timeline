Summary

Nehemiah receives permission from Persia to rebuild Jerusalem's walls. Despite opposition, the work is completed and the city gains renewed security.

Nehemiah hears that Jerusalem's walls are broken and prays before asking the Persian king for permission to go. In Jerusalem he organizes the work, answers opposition, and keeps the people focused.

The wall is completed in a remarkably short period, strengthening the restored community. Nehemiah's leadership combines prayer, planning, courage, and public accountability.

Historical Background

Historical reconstruction: Nehemiah served under Artaxerxes I, and the wall rebuilding is commonly dated to 445 BC. Archaeological evidence: Persian-period remains in Jerusalem are debated, but the city was smaller and more fragile than in the late monarchy.
