Summary

Solomon asks God for wisdom to govern Israel rather than for long life or wealth. God grants wisdom and also gives riches and honor.

At Gibeon, Solomon recognizes his youth and the weight of ruling God's people. He asks for an understanding heart to discern between good and evil, and God approves the request.

The famous judgment between two women demonstrates practical wisdom in public justice. Solomon's reign begins with divine generosity, but the gift also creates responsibility to walk faithfully.

Historical Background

Scholarly opinion: Solomon's wisdom traditions are linked with Israel's later wisdom literature. Historical reconstruction: Ancient Near Eastern kings were expected to embody justice, and 1 Kings presents Solomon's wisdom as a divine gift for that task.
