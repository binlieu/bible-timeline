Summary

David rules Israel from Jerusalem and receives the covenant promise of an enduring dynasty.

David is anointed king over all Israel and establishes Jerusalem as his capital. His reign marks the high point of the united monarchy and shapes Israel's worship, politics, and hope.

In 2 Samuel 7, God promises David a house and kingdom, creating the expectation of a future Davidic king.

Historical Background

David is commonly placed in the early tenth century BC. Archaeological discussions continue around the scale of the united monarchy.
