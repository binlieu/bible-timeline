Summary

The returned exiles rebuild the altar and eventually complete the second temple. Prophets Haggai and Zechariah encourage the people to finish the work despite opposition and delay.

The returnees first restore sacrifice, then lay the temple foundation amid both joy and grief. Opposition and discouragement halt progress until Haggai and Zechariah call the community back to obedience.

The temple is completed in 516 BC, giving the restored community a renewed center for worship. It is smaller than Solomon's temple, but it marks a major act of covenant restoration after exile.

Historical Background

Historical reconstruction: The second temple was completed in the sixth year of Darius I, commonly dated 516 BC. Scholarly opinion: Ezra 3-6 combines local restoration memory with Persian administrative correspondence.
