Summary

Sennacherib of Assyria invades Judah and threatens Jerusalem during Hezekiah's reign. Hezekiah seeks the Lord, Isaiah delivers God's word, and Jerusalem is spared.

Assyria captures fortified cities of Judah and sends officials to intimidate Jerusalem. Hezekiah brings the threat before the Lord, and Isaiah announces that the Assyrian king will not take the city.

The deliverance preserves Jerusalem but leaves Judah deeply damaged. The event becomes one of the clearest points where biblical narrative and external imperial records overlap.

Historical Background

Archaeological evidence: Sennacherib's prism records his 701 BC campaign and says he shut Hezekiah in Jerusalem like a bird in a cage. Archaeological evidence: The Lachish reliefs depict the Assyrian siege of Lachish, matching the campaign context in 2 Kings.
