Summary

The queen of Sheba visits Solomon to test his wisdom with hard questions. She praises the Lord when she sees Solomon's wisdom, wealth, and ordered kingdom.

The queen arrives with a large caravan and questions Solomon extensively. The narrative presents Solomon's wisdom and prosperity as internationally recognized, fulfilling the promise of honor given in 1 Kings 3.

Her praise directs attention beyond Solomon to the Lord who loved Israel and set him on the throne. The episode is a high point before the account turns to the dangers of wealth, horses, and foreign alliances.

Historical Background

Historical reconstruction: Sheba is commonly associated with South Arabian trade networks, though the exact identity of the queen is not independently attested. Scholarly opinion: The account reflects the prestige of long-distance trade and royal diplomacy.
