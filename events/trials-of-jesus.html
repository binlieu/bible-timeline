Summary

Jesus is examined by Jewish leaders and by Pilate, who sentences him to crucifixion under public pressure. Peter denies Jesus, and Judas' betrayal ends in despair.

Historical Background

Archaeological evidence: The Pilate stone anchors Pilate in first-century Judea, and the Caiaphas ossuary supports the historical setting of the priestly leadership named in the Gospels. Scholarly opinion: Details of the hearings are discussed in relation to Jewish and Roman legal practice, but the convergence on Pilate, priestly opposition, and execution is historically significant. Historical reconstruction: The trials move from temple leadership to Roman authority because only Rome could carry out capital punishment in this political setting.
