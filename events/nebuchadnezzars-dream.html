Summary

Daniel interprets Nebuchadnezzar's dream of a great statue and a stone that becomes a mountain. The dream declares that earthly kingdoms rise and fall under God's rule.

Nebuchadnezzar demands both the dream and its interpretation, exposing the limits of Babylon's wise men. Daniel prays with his friends, receives the mystery from God, and gives credit to the God who reveals secrets.

The dream places Babylon within a larger sequence of kingdoms and announces a kingdom established by God. In exile, Daniel's interpretation teaches that imperial power is real but not ultimate.

Historical Background

Scholarly opinion: Interpretations of the statue's kingdoms vary, but the central claim is clear: God rules over history. Historical reconstruction: Babylonian courts valued divination and dream interpretation, making Daniel's role culturally recognizable.
