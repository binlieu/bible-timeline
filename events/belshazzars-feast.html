Summary

Belshazzar profanes vessels from the Jerusalem temple during a feast. Daniel interprets the writing on the wall, and Babylon falls that night.

Belshazzar's feast treats the holy vessels as trophies for idol praise. The mysterious writing announces that his kingdom has been weighed and given to the Medes and Persians.

Daniel's interpretation marks the end of Babylonian dominance in the book. The fall opens the way for Persian rule and the later decree allowing return.

Historical Background

Historical reconstruction: Babylon fell to Cyrus in 539 BC. Archaeological evidence: Babylonian records identify Belshazzar as the son of Nabonidus who exercised royal authority, explaining his role in Daniel's court setting.
