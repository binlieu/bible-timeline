Summary

Elijah confronts Ahab, the prophets of Baal, and Israel on Mount Carmel. The Lord answers by fire, exposing Baal as powerless and calling Israel back to covenant loyalty.

Elijah gathers Israel and asks why they are limping between two opinions. The prophets of Baal cry out without answer, but Elijah repairs the Lord's altar, prays, and fire falls from heaven.

The event is a covenant confrontation, not a contest of technique. It exposes the religious crisis under Ahab and Jezebel and prepares for Elijah's flight, despair, and renewed commission.

Historical Background

Historical reconstruction: Mount Carmel overlooked territories influenced by Phoenician Baal worship, making it a fitting site for the confrontation. Scholarly opinion: The account is theological narrative focused on exclusive worship of the Lord.
