Summary

Solomon's foreign marriages turn his heart after other gods. God announces that the kingdom will be torn from his house, though one tribe will remain for David's sake.

Solomon's later reign contradicts the covenant warnings for Israel's king. Foreign alliances and idolatrous worship draw his heart away from the Lord, and adversaries rise against him.

The prophet Ahijah announces that Jeroboam will receive ten tribes after Solomon's death. The united kingdom ends not simply because of politics but because covenant unfaithfulness fractures the royal house.

Historical Background

Historical reconstruction: Solomon's reign likely involved extensive labor, taxation, diplomacy, and trade, factors that help explain later northern resentment. Scholarly opinion: 1 Kings interprets the division chiefly through covenant faithfulness and idolatry.
