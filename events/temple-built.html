Summary

Solomon builds and dedicates the temple in Jerusalem. The ark is placed inside, and Solomon prays that God will hear his people when they pray toward this house.

Solomon begins building the temple in the fourth year of his reign, using costly materials and skilled labor. The temple gives permanent architectural expression to Israel's worship, though Solomon confesses that heaven itself cannot contain God.

At the dedication, the glory of the Lord fills the house, and Solomon prays for mercy, justice, forgiveness, and restoration. The prayer anticipates exile and return, making the temple both a place of worship and a witness to covenant accountability.

Historical Background

Historical reconstruction: The first temple stood in Jerusalem until the Babylonian destruction in 586 BC. Scholarly opinion: Its exact architectural details are known from biblical description; no uncontested remains of Solomon's temple are available for direct excavation.
