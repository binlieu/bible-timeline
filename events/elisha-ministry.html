Summary

Elisha ministers in the northern kingdom through miracles, counsel, and prophetic warnings. His work touches kings, widows, soldiers, foreigners, and the suffering people of Israel.

Elisha's ministry spans several reigns in Israel and includes acts of provision, healing, resurrection, and military counsel. The healing of Naaman shows that the God of Israel is not limited by national borders or social rank.

The duration of the ministry gives the books of Kings a long prophetic witness in the north. Elisha's signs show mercy while his warnings expose the kingdom's continuing instability and unbelief.

Historical Background

Historical reconstruction: The Aramean conflicts in the Elisha narratives fit the ninth-century struggle between Israel and Damascus. Scholarly opinion: The miracle cycles preserve prophetic traditions centered on the northern kingdom.
