Summary

Ezra the priest and scribe returns to Jerusalem with authority to teach the law. He leads reforms aimed at covenant faithfulness among the restored community.

Ezra comes from Babylon to Jerusalem with Persian authorization and a mission centered on the law of Moses. His calling is to study, practice, and teach the law in Israel.

The reforms in Ezra 9-10 address covenant compromise after the return. The episode is difficult and sobering, showing that restoration from exile still requires holiness and communal obedience.

Historical Background

Historical reconstruction: Ezra's return is commonly dated to 458 BC during the reign of Artaxerxes I. Scholarly opinion: Ezra represents the growing importance of scribal teaching and Torah-centered community identity in the postexilic period.
