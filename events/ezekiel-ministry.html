Summary

Ezekiel ministers among the exiles in Babylon before and after Jerusalem's fall. He announces judgment, explains the exile, and promises restoration, a new heart, and a renewed temple vision.

Ezekiel is called by the Kebar canal and sees the glory of the Lord far from Jerusalem. His ministry spans the years around the city's destruction, confronting idolatry and false hope among the exiles.

After news of Jerusalem's fall, Ezekiel's message turns strongly toward restoration. He promises renewed shepherding, a new heart and spirit, national resurrection imagery, and a final vision of ordered worship.

Historical Background

Historical reconstruction: Ezekiel dates several visions, beginning in 593 BC among Judean exiles in Babylonia. Scholarly opinion: His ministry bridges priestly concerns, prophetic judgment, and restoration hope during the exile.
