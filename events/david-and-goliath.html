Summary

David confronts Goliath in the Valley of Elah while Saul and Israel fear the Philistine champion. David defeats him in the name of the Lord, and Israel wins a major victory.

Goliath challenges Israel's army, and David interprets the crisis as defiance against the living God. Refusing Saul's armor, David uses a sling and stone and then strikes down the Philistine champion.

The victory reveals David's faith and courage, but it also begins the fame that will provoke Saul's jealousy. The event is therefore both a deliverance story and the first public step toward David's rise.

Historical Background

Historical reconstruction: The Valley of Elah lay near the border zone where Israelite and Philistine forces contested control. Archaeological evidence: Excavations in the Shephelah show fortified Iron Age settlements in the wider region, though the individual combat itself is known from Scripture.
