Summary

Daniel and his friends are taken to Babylon and trained for royal service. They remain faithful in diet and worship, and God gives them wisdom.

Daniel 1 opens the exile by showing young Judeans serving in Babylon without surrendering covenant identity. Their refusal of the royal food tests whether faithfulness can survive away from the land and temple.

God gives Daniel and his friends wisdom and favor, placing them in positions where they will bear witness before imperial rulers. The event introduces the exilic question of how God's people live faithfully under foreign power.

Historical Background

Historical reconstruction: The first Babylonian deportations from Judah began before Jerusalem's final destruction. Scholarly opinion: Daniel 1 is set in the early Babylonian period and frames the court stories that follow.
