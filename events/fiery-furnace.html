Summary

Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego refuse to worship Nebuchadnezzar's image. God preserves them in the furnace, and the king acknowledges their deliverer.

The three Judean officials refuse idolatrous worship even under threat of death. Their answer leaves room for either deliverance or martyrdom, but not for compromise.

When they are thrown into the furnace, a fourth figure appears with them and they emerge unharmed. The story shows covenant loyalty under pressure and God's power to save in exile.

Historical Background

Historical reconstruction: Imperial images and loyalty ceremonies were common tools of royal authority. Scholarly opinion: The account functions as a model of faithful resistance to idolatrous state power.
