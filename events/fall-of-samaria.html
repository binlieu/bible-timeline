Summary

Assyria captures Samaria and exiles the northern kingdom of Israel. The biblical writer explains the fall as the result of persistent idolatry and refusal to heed the prophets.

After years of Assyrian pressure, Samaria falls and many Israelites are deported. Foreign populations are settled in the land, reshaping the region that later becomes associated with the Samaritans.

2 Kings 17 gives a theological explanation: Israel feared other gods, rejected the covenant, and ignored prophetic warnings. The northern kingdom ends as a political state, while Judah remains under growing Assyrian threat.

Historical Background

Archaeological evidence: Assyrian royal inscriptions describe campaigns and deportations connected with the fall of Samaria. Historical reconstruction: The fall is dated to 722 BC, during the transition from Shalmaneser V to Sargon II.
