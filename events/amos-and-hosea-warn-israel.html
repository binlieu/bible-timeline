Summary

Amos and Hosea warn the northern kingdom that injustice, idolatry, and covenant betrayal will bring judgment. Their messages also hold out hope for restoration beyond punishment.

Amos denounces oppression, false worship, and complacency during a period of prosperity. Hosea portrays Israel's unfaithfulness through the language of a broken marriage, calling the people to return to the Lord.

Together these prophets interpret Israel's decline before Assyria not as bad luck but as covenant judgment. Their ministry spans years of warning before Samaria finally falls.

Historical Background

Historical reconstruction: The eighth century BC brought Assyrian expansion and pressure on Israel and Judah. Scholarly opinion: Amos and Hosea are among the earliest writing prophets and preserve major evidence for northern Israel's religious crisis.
