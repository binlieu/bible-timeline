Summary

Esther becomes queen in Persia and risks her life to intercede for her people. Haman's plot is overturned, and the Jews are delivered from destruction.

Esther lives in the Persian court under Ahasuerus, traditionally identified with Xerxes I. When Haman plots against the Jews, Mordecai urges Esther to act, and she approaches the king at great personal risk.

The reversal of Haman's plan leads to Jewish deliverance and the celebration of Purim. The book never names God directly, yet it portrays providence working through timing, courage, and reversal.

Historical Background

Historical reconstruction: Susa was a major Persian royal city, and Ahasuerus is commonly identified with Xerxes I. Scholarly opinion: Esther reflects Jewish life in diaspora and the vulnerability of minority communities under imperial power.
