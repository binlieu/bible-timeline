Summary

Josiah repairs the temple, hears the Book of the Law, and leads Judah in covenant renewal. He removes idolatrous practices and celebrates Passover, though judgment on Judah is not fully turned away.

The discovery of the law during temple repairs leads Josiah to humble himself before the Lord. He gathers the people, renews the covenant, and carries out reforms against unauthorized worship.

Josiah's reforms are the last major covenant renewal before Judah's fall. The narrative honors his wholehearted obedience while still showing that generations of sin have brought Judah near judgment.

Historical Background

Historical reconstruction: Josiah's reform fits the late seventh-century weakening of Assyrian control and renewed Judean independence. Scholarly opinion: Many scholars connect the discovered law with Deuteronomic covenant traditions.
