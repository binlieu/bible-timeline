Summary

Jesus drives merchants and money changers from the temple courts and calls the temple a house of prayer. The action challenges corrupt worship.

Historical Background

Archaeological evidence: Excavations around the Temple Mount and first-century Jerusalem illuminate the scale of pilgrimage traffic, money exchange, and sacrificial activity. Scholarly opinion: Scholars debate whether John and the Synoptics describe one temple action or separate actions, but the act is consistently tied to Jesus' prophetic confrontation with the temple establishment. Historical reconstruction: The action belongs naturally in Passover week, when public temple symbolism carried maximum force.
