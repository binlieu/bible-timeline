Summary

God judges widespread human corruption with a flood and preserves Noah's family through the ark.

Human violence and corruption fill the earth, and God announces judgment through a great flood. Noah is described as righteous in his generation and obeys God's command to build the ark.

After the flood, God establishes a covenant with Noah, promising not to destroy all flesh by flood again and giving the rainbow as the sign.

Historical Background

Flood traditions appear in several ancient Near Eastern texts, though Genesis gives the event a distinct covenantal and moral focus.
