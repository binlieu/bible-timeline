Summary

David brings the ark of the covenant to Jerusalem with public worship. The event marks Jerusalem as both royal capital and central worship location.

David first attempts to bring the ark to Jerusalem but the death of Uzzah halts the procession and teaches the seriousness of holy worship. Afterward the ark is brought with sacrifices, music, and rejoicing.

David's dancing before the Lord shows royal humility before Israel's true King. The ark's arrival links Jerusalem's political role to covenant worship and prepares for the later temple.

Historical Background

Historical reconstruction: Moving the ark to Jerusalem strengthened the city's national and religious role. Scholarly opinion: The narrative emphasizes ordered worship and covenant legitimacy more than administrative detail.
