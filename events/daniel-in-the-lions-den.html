Summary

Daniel continues praying to God despite a royal prohibition and is thrown into a lions' den. God preserves him, and the king publicly honors Daniel's God.

Daniel's enemies exploit his faithfulness by persuading the king to issue a law against petitioning anyone but the king. Daniel keeps his regular pattern of prayer, making his loyalty visible rather than defensive.

God shuts the lions' mouths, and Daniel is vindicated. The story continues the exile theme that faithful witness can endure under changing empires.

Historical Background

Scholarly opinion: The identity of Darius the Mede is debated, with proposals ranging from a title or governor to a literary designation for early Persian rule. Historical reconstruction: The story is placed at the transition from Babylonian to Medo-Persian authority.
