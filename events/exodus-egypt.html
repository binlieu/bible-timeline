Summary

God delivers Israel from slavery in Egypt through Moses after the Passover judgment.

After generations in Egypt, Israel is enslaved and cries out to God. Through Moses, God confronts Pharaoh with signs and judgments, culminating in the Passover.

Israel leaves Egypt as a redeemed people, beginning the journey toward Sinai and the land promised to Abraham.

Historical Background

Traditional early-date chronology places the exodus in the fifteenth century BC, while other chronologies propose later dates.
