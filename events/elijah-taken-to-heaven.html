Summary

Elijah crosses the Jordan with Elisha and is taken up by a whirlwind. Elisha receives Elijah's mantle and begins his prophetic ministry.

Elisha refuses to leave Elijah as they travel from Gilgal to Bethel, Jericho, and the Jordan. After Elijah is taken, Elisha takes up the mantle and strikes the water, showing continuity in prophetic authority.

The transition joins judgment and hope: the prophetic word will continue in Israel even after Elijah's departure. Elisha's ministry follows with signs of mercy, warning, and judgment.

Historical Background

Historical reconstruction: The Jordan crossing deliberately echoes earlier acts of divine power in Israel's story. Scholarly opinion: The account presents prophetic succession through divine authorization rather than institutional appointment.
