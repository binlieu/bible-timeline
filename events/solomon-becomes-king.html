Summary

As David nears death, Solomon is anointed king in Jerusalem. The succession secures the Davidic line after Adonijah's failed attempt to claim the throne.

Adonijah attempts to make himself king while David is old and weak, but Bathsheba and Nathan appeal to David's promise concerning Solomon. David orders Solomon anointed at Gihon, and the city receives him as king.

David charges Solomon to walk in the Lord's ways and to govern decisively. The transition preserves the Davidic covenant line but also exposes the dangers of court rivalry around succession.

Historical Background

Historical reconstruction: Royal succession often involved public anointing, military support, and control of the capital. Scholarly opinion: The narrative frames Solomon's accession as legitimate because it rests on David's command and prophetic support.
