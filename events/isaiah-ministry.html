Summary

Isaiah ministers in Judah across the reigns of Uzziah, Jotham, Ahaz, and Hezekiah. He announces judgment, calls for trust in the Lord, and gives sweeping promises of restoration and a coming servant.

Isaiah's ministry spans decades of Assyrian pressure, royal crisis, and spiritual compromise in Judah. He warns against empty religion and foreign dependence while calling the people to holiness and faith.

The book also looks beyond immediate judgment to comfort, return, and the Lord's servant. Its long horizon connects eighth-century Judah with exile, restoration, and later messianic hope.

Historical Background

Historical reconstruction: Isaiah's call is dated to the year King Uzziah died, commonly placed around 740 BC. Scholarly opinion: The book contains material addressing multiple historical horizons, including Assyrian crisis, Babylonian exile, and restoration hope.
