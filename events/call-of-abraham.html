Summary

God calls Abram to leave his country and promises land, descendants, and blessing to all families of the earth.

Abram is called from his homeland to a land God will show him. The promises of land, great nationhood, blessing, and worldwide blessing become a central thread in biblical history.

Abram obeys and travels to Canaan, building altars as he moves through the land. The patriarchal narrative develops God's covenant faithfulness through his family.

Historical Background

Traditional chronology places Abraham in the early second millennium BC, though exact dating is debated.
