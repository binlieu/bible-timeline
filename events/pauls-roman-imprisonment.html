Historical Background

Archaeological evidence: Rome's first-century Jewish community, roads, ports, and imperial administration fit Acts' closing setting. Scholarly opinion: Acts ends before narrating Paul's death, so the house-arrest period is usually dated around AD 60-62. Historical reconstruction: Paul's appeal to Caesar explains the transfer from provincial custody to supervised residence in Rome.
