Summary

After Saul's death, David is first made king over Judah at Hebron and later over all Israel. He captures Jerusalem and makes it his capital.

David mourns Saul and Jonathan, then begins ruling Judah from Hebron. After conflict with Saul's remaining house, the tribes of Israel come to David and acknowledge him as king.

David takes Jerusalem, a strategically placed Jebusite stronghold, and establishes it as the political center of the united kingdom. This move gives Israel a capital not previously identified with one northern or southern tribe.

Historical Background

Archaeological evidence: The Tel Dan stele refers to the House of David, providing important extrabiblical evidence for a Davidic dynasty. Scholarly opinion: The scale of David's kingdom is debated, but Jerusalem became the enduring center of Judahite kingship.
