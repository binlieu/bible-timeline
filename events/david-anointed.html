Summary

Samuel goes to Bethlehem and anoints David, the youngest son of Jesse. The Spirit of the Lord comes upon David while Saul's reign begins to unravel.

God sends Samuel to Jesse's house in Bethlehem, where outward appearance is set aside and David is chosen. The anointing is quiet and local, not yet a public transfer of the throne.

David then enters Saul's orbit as a musician, bringing relief to the troubled king. The chapter places the rejected king and the chosen king side by side, preparing for the long conflict before David actually rules.

Historical Background

Scholarly opinion: David's early life is known mainly from biblical narrative, while later external evidence supports the historical memory of a Davidic royal house. Historical reconstruction: Bethlehem was a small Judean town, fitting the narrative's emphasis on unexpected selection.
