Summary

Malachi addresses the restored community after temple worship has resumed. He rebukes corrupt worship, covenant unfaithfulness, and spiritual cynicism while promising the coming day of the Lord.

Malachi's ministry belongs to the later postexilic period, when the temple stands but devotion has grown careless. Priests offer blemished sacrifices, the people question God's justice, and covenant faithfulness is weak.

The book closes the Old Testament prophetic witness by calling for covenant remembrance and future hope. It anticipates a messenger who will prepare the way before the Lord comes.

Historical Background

Scholarly opinion: Malachi is usually dated to the fifth century BC because its concerns fit the Persian-period restored community. Historical reconstruction: The ministry likely followed the rebuilding of the temple and overlaps the kinds of issues addressed by Ezra and Nehemiah.
