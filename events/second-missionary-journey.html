Historical Background

Archaeological evidence: The Delphi/Gallio inscription anchors Gallio's proconsulship in Achaia around AD 51-52, giving a key chronological fixed point for Acts 18 in Corinth. Scholarly opinion: This is one of the strongest external anchors for Pauline chronology. Historical reconstruction: Paul's Corinth ministry is therefore placed around AD 50-52, with surrounding travel dated accordingly.
