Summary

Cyrus of Persia issues a decree allowing the Jews to return and rebuild the temple in Jerusalem. The return from exile begins under Persian rule.

Ezra opens by connecting Cyrus's decree to the word of the Lord spoken by Jeremiah. Cyrus authorizes return, temple rebuilding, and the restoration of temple vessels taken by Nebuchadnezzar.

The decree does not end all hardship, but it begins the restoration period. Exiles return in stages, rebuild worship, and live as a covenant community under imperial oversight.

Historical Background

Archaeological evidence: The Cyrus Cylinder shows Cyrus's broader policy of restoring sanctuaries and repatriating divine images, though it does not name Judah. Historical reconstruction: The decree is dated to 538 BC, shortly after Persia took Babylon.
