Summary

Humanity gathers to build a city and tower, and God confuses their language and scatters them.

The people of Babel seek to make a name for themselves and avoid being scattered across the earth. Their project becomes a symbol of unified human pride apart from God.

God confuses their language, halting the project and scattering the peoples. The narrative prepares for God's call of Abram and the promise to bless all nations.

Historical Background

Babel is associated with Babylon and the Mesopotamian world, where temple towers known as ziggurats were prominent.
