Summary

After Solomon dies, Rehoboam rejects the elders' counsel and the northern tribes follow Jeroboam. Israel and Judah become separate kingdoms.

Rehoboam's harsh answer to the northern tribes turns their grievance into revolt. Jeroboam becomes king over Israel, while Rehoboam continues the Davidic line in Judah.

Jeroboam establishes alternate worship centers to keep the north from returning to Jerusalem. The division becomes the political and spiritual setting for the ministries of Elijah, Elisha, Amos, Hosea, Isaiah, Jeremiah, and later prophets.

Historical Background

Historical reconstruction: The division reflects regional tensions, taxation, and forced labor after Solomon. Scholarly opinion: The biblical account interprets those pressures through Solomon's idolatry and Rehoboam's folly.
