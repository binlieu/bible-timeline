Summary

Cain murders Abel after God accepts Abel's offering and warns Cain about sin.

Genesis 4 traces sin's spread from disobedience to violence. Cain becomes angry when God regards Abel's offering and not his own.

God warns Cain that sin is crouching at the door, but Cain kills his brother. Cain is judged and sent out, yet God protects him from vengeance.
