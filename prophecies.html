Prophecies

Prophecies with subjects, fulfillments, and theme links.

Messianic

A Great Light in Galilee

Isaiah 9:1–2

The land of Zebulun and Naphtali, Galilee of the nations, once in gloom, will see a great light.

A Priest Forever after Melchizedek

Psalm 110:1–4

'The LORD says to my Lord: sit at my right hand'; 'you are a priest forever after the order of Melchizedek.'

An Everlasting Throne for David's House

2 Samuel 7:12–16

God will raise up David's offspring, establish his kingdom, and make his throne endure forever.

Blessing to All Nations through Abraham's Seed

Genesis 12:3; 22:18

In Abraham and his offspring all the families of the earth will be blessed.

Not Abandoned to the Grave

Psalm 16:8–11

'You will not abandon my soul to Sheol, or let your holy one see corruption.'

Not One of His Bones Broken

Psalm 34:20; Exodus 12:46

The righteous one's bones are kept — not one broken; the Passover lamb's bones must not be broken.

Out of Egypt I Called My Son

Hosea 11:1

'When Israel was a child, I loved him, and out of Egypt I called my son' — spoken of the exodus.

The Immanuel Sign

Isaiah 7:14

The virgin (Heb.

The King Comes on a Donkey

Zechariah 9:9

Zion's king comes righteous and victorious, humble and mounted on a donkey, on a colt.

The Psalm of the Forsaken One

Psalm 22

'My God, my God, why have you forsaken me?' — mocked by onlookers, hands and feet pierced, garments divided by lot; yet ending in praise before the nations.

The Ruler from Bethlehem

Micah 5:2

From Bethlehem Ephrathah, little among Judah's clans, will come forth a ruler whose origins are from of old.

The Seed of the Woman

Genesis 3:15

The offspring of the woman will crush the serpent's head while being struck himself — read from earliest times as the first gospel promise (the protoevangelium).

The Seventy Weeks

Daniel 9:24–27

Seventy 'weeks' are decreed: from the word to restore Jerusalem until an anointed one, atonement made, and desolations decreed.

The Suffering Servant

Isaiah 52:13–53:12

The servant is despised, pierced for our transgressions, silent before his shearers, numbered with transgressors, buried with the rich, and yet will see his offspring and justify many.

The Voice in the Wilderness

Isaiah 40:3; Malachi 3:1; 4:5–6

A voice cries: prepare the way of the LORD; the messenger comes before him, in the spirit of Elijah.

They Will Look on Him Whom They Pierced

Zechariah 12:10

They will look on me, on him whom they have pierced, and mourn as for an only child.

Thirty Pieces of Silver

Zechariah 11:12–13

The shepherd's wage — thirty pieces of silver — is thrown to the potter in the house of the LORD.

National & historical

Cyrus Named as Restorer

Isaiah 44:28–45:13

God names Cyrus as his 'shepherd' and 'anointed' who will subdue nations and order Jerusalem and the temple rebuilt.

Four Hundred Years in a Foreign Land

Genesis 15:13–16

Abram's offspring will be sojourners and slaves in a land not theirs four hundred years, then come out with great possessions and return in the fourth generation.

Jerusalem Given to Babylon

Jeremiah 25:8–11; 32:28–29; Ezekiel 24:1–14

Because Judah would not listen, Nebuchadnezzar will take the city; it will be burned and the land left desolate.

Josiah Named at Bethel's Altar

1 Kings 13:1–3

Against Jeroboam's new altar at Bethel: a son named Josiah, born to the house of David, will defile this altar — some three centuries in advance.

Sennacherib Will Not Enter the City

2 Kings 19:20–34; Isaiah 37:33–35

The king of Assyria will not enter Jerusalem or shoot an arrow there; he will return the way he came.

Seventy Years for Babylon

Jeremiah 25:11–12; 29:10

Judah will serve Babylon seventy years; then Babylon will be punished and the exiles brought home.

The Fall of the Northern Kingdom

Amos 5:27; 7:17; Hosea 10:5–8; 13:16

Israel will go into exile beyond Damascus; Samaria's calf will be carried to Assyria and her king cut off.

The Flood Announced

Genesis 6:13–21

God announces the end of all flesh and instructs Noah to build the ark — 'a herald of righteousness' to his generation.

The Four Kingdoms

Daniel 2:31–45; 7:1–27

A succession of four kingdoms — gold, silver, bronze, iron — after which the God of heaven sets up a kingdom that will never be destroyed.

The New Covenant

Jeremiah 31:31–34

Days are coming when God will make a new covenant — law written on hearts, sins remembered no more.

The Oracle against Tyre

Ezekiel 26

Many nations will come against Tyre; her stones and timber will be cast into the sea and her site scraped bare like a rock.

The Spirit Poured on All Flesh

Joel 2:28–32

Afterward God will pour out his Spirit on all flesh — sons and daughters prophesying — and everyone who calls on the name of the LORD will be saved.

Spoken by Jesus

Before the Rooster Crows

Matthew 26:34; John 13:38

Before the rooster crows, Peter will deny Jesus three times.

Not One Stone upon Another

Matthew 24:1–2; Luke 19:41–44; 21:5–6, 20–24

Of the temple buildings: not one stone will be left upon another; Jerusalem will be surrounded by armies and trodden down.

The Son of Man Raised on the Third Day

Matthew 16:21; 17:22–23; 20:18–19; John 2:19–22

The Son of Man must suffer, be killed, and on the third day be raised; 'destroy this temple, and in three days I will raise it up.'
