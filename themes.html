Themes

Biblical themes connected to events, people, and prophecies.

All Themes

Covenant

God binds himself to his people by solemn promise: preservation to Noah, land and offspring and blessing to Abraham, law and belonging at Sinai, an everlasting throne to David, and the new covenant of the heart promised through Jeremiah and claimed at the Last Supper.

Exile and Return

Banishment and homecoming as the Bible's recurring shape: Eden lost, Egypt and exodus, Babylon and the return under Cyrus — and the New Testament's claim that in Christ the true return from exile begins.

Faith and Doubt

Abraham believing against hope, Israel doubting at Kadesh, Gideon's fleece, Elijah's despair, Peter sinking and denying, Thomas demanding to see — and faith counted as righteousness throughout.

Judgment and Mercy

The flood and the ark, Sodom and Lot's rescue, exile and return, Nineveh spared at Jonah's preaching: judgment announced, and mercy triumphing wherever there is repentance — 'in wrath remember mercy.'

Law and Grace

The commandments given at Sinai as covenant charter, Israel's long failure to keep them, and the promise of a law written on the heart — the tension the New Testament resolves in 'grace and truth through Jesus Christ.'

Messiah

The anointed one promised across the whole canon: the seed of the woman, the blessing of Abraham, the prophet like Moses, the son of David, the suffering servant, the ruler from Bethlehem — a hope the New Testament proclaims fulfilled in Jesus.

Redemption

God buys back what was lost: Israel out of Egypt by the blood of the lamb, Ruth by her kinsman-redeemer, the exiles out of Babylon, and — in the New Testament's proclamation — sinners by the blood of Christ.

Resurrection and Life

God's power over death: sons restored by Elijah and Elisha, Israel's national resurrection in Ezekiel's valley of bones, Daniel's awakening of those who sleep in the dust, Lazarus called from the tomb, and the empty tomb on the third day — 'the firstfruits of those who have fallen asleep.'

Sacrifice and Atonement

Sin costs life, and God provides the substitute: the ram in Isaac's place, the Passover lamb, the Day of Atonement, the servant who bears the sin of many, and 'the Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world.'

The Kingdom of God

God's reign asserted over all rival kingdoms: promised to David's line, dramatized in Daniel's visions of empires overthrown, announced by Jesus as 'at hand,' taught in parables, and consummated in the vision of Revelation.

The Presence of God

God dwelling with his people: Eden, the burning bush, the pillar of cloud and fire, the tabernacle and temple filled with glory, the glory departing before the exile, the Word made flesh who 'tabernacled' among us, the Spirit at Pentecost, and the city where God is all in all.

The Remnant

God preserves a faithful few through every judgment: Noah's household, the seven thousand who never bowed to Baal, the survivors of exile who return, and the remnant chosen by grace of Paul's argument.

The Shepherd and the Flock

From the shepherd-king David and the LORD as shepherd of Psalm 23, through the failed shepherds of Ezekiel 34 and the promise that God himself will seek the lost, to Jesus the good shepherd who lays down his life for the sheep.
