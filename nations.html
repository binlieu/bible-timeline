Nations

Nations and kingdoms connected to the Bible timeline.

All Nations

throughout the Old Testament period

Ammon

The people east of the Jordan descended from Lot, centered at Rabbah.

dominant conflict approx. 950–732 BC

Aram (Syria)

The Aramean kingdoms centered on Damascus: subdued by David, then Israel's persistent northern rival — the wars of Ahab's era, Naaman the healed commander, and Hazael's oppression, until Assyria took Damascus in 732 BC.

dominant approx. 900–612 BC

Assyria

The Mesopotamian empire from Nineveh whose armies destroyed the northern kingdom (722 BC) and devastated Judah under Sennacherib (701 BC).

dominant approx. 626–539 BC

Babylon

The Neo-Babylonian empire of Nebuchadnezzar II, God's declared instrument of judgment on Judah: three deportations (605, 597, 586 BC), the temple burned, and the exile in which Daniel served and Ezekiel prophesied.

before and during the conquest, approx. 2100–1000 BC

Canaan (the Canaanite peoples)

The peoples of the land promised to Abraham — Canaanites, Amorites, Hittites, Hivites, Jebusites, and others — whose iniquity was 'not yet complete' in Abraham's day (Genesis 15:16).

throughout the Old Testament period

Edom

The nation descended from Esau, in the red mountains south of the Dead Sea.

throughout the biblical period

Egypt

The great Nile kingdom: refuge in famine for Abraham and Jacob's family, then the house of bondage whose Pharaoh (unnamed in the text) enslaved Israel until the ten plagues and the exodus.

dominant 331–63 BC; cultural influence through the NT era

Greece

Alexander's empire and its successor kingdoms, which Hellenized the biblical world between the Testaments — the Septuagint, the Maccabean crisis under the Seleucids, and the Greek language of the New Testament.

approx. 930–722 BC

Israel (Northern Kingdom)

The ten northern tribes that broke from the house of David under Jeroboam I, with capitals at Shechem, Tirzah, and finally Samaria.

approx. 1050–930 BC (united monarchy); the people from Jacob onward

Israel (United Kingdom)

The covenant people descended from Jacob (renamed Israel), organized as twelve tribes, delivered from Egypt, and united as one kingdom under Saul, David, and Solomon with Jerusalem as capital from David's reign.

approx. 930–586 BC

Judah (Southern Kingdom)

The southern kingdom under the unbroken Davidic dynasty at Jerusalem, comprising Judah and Benjamin.

throughout the Old Testament period

Moab

The people east of the Dead Sea descended from Lot.

dominant 539–331 BC

Persia (Medo-Persia)

The empire of Cyrus the Great and his successors, which overthrew Babylon and reversed its policy of exile: Cyrus decreed the return and temple rebuilding (538 BC), Darius confirmed it, Xerxes (Ahasuerus) reigned in Esther's day, and Artaxerxes commissioned Ezra and Nehemiah.

dominant conflict approx. 1200–960 BC

Philistia

The five-city league of the southern coast (Gaza, Ashkelon, Ashdod, Ekron, Gath), Israel's chief rival in the days of the judges and Saul: Samson's exploits, the ark's capture, and Goliath of Gath.

throughout the biblical period

Phoenicia (Tyre and Sidon)

The maritime cities of the northern coast.

dominant from 63 BC through the New Testament era

Rome

The empire ruling the biblical world of the New Testament: Pompey took Jerusalem in 63 BC, Herod ruled as client king, a census brought Joseph and Mary to Bethlehem, a Roman prefect crucified Jesus, Roman roads and peace carried the gospel, and Roman armies destroyed the temple in AD 70.
