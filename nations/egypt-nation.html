Egypt

The great Nile kingdom: refuge in famine for Abraham and Jacob's family, then the house of bondage whose Pharaoh (unnamed in the text) enslaved Israel until the ten plagues and the exodus. Later a tempting but unreliable ally against Assyria and Babylon, and a refuge again for the infant Jesus.

Historical Arc

Humbled in the exodus; conquered successively by Assyria, Babylon, Persia, Greece, and Rome, as the prophets had declared (Ezekiel 29–32).
