Aram (Syria)

The Aramean kingdoms centered on Damascus: subdued by David, then Israel's persistent northern rival — the wars of Ahab's era, Naaman the healed commander, and Hazael's oppression, until Assyria took Damascus in 732 BC. Aramaic became the common tongue of the later biblical world.

Historical Arc

Damascus fell to Tiglath-pileser III in 732 BC, as Amos 1:3–5 and Isaiah 17 declared.
