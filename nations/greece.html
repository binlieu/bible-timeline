Greece

Alexander's empire and its successor kingdoms, which Hellenized the biblical world between the Testaments — the Septuagint, the Maccabean crisis under the Seleucids, and the Greek language of the New Testament. Daniel's visions of the goat and the third kingdom are commonly read of Greece; Paul preached in its great cities.

Historical Arc

Successor kingdoms absorbed by Rome (Macedonia 146 BC; Egypt 30 BC); Greek culture and language endured as the medium of the gospel.
