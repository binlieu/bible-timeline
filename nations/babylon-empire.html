Babylon

The Neo-Babylonian empire of Nebuchadnezzar II, God's declared instrument of judgment on Judah: three deportations (605, 597, 586 BC), the temple burned, and the exile in which Daniel served and Ezekiel prophesied. In Scripture Babylon also becomes the enduring symbol of human pride from Babel to Revelation.

Historical Arc

Fell to Cyrus of Persia in a single night in 539 BC (Daniel 5), as Isaiah and Jeremiah had foretold.
