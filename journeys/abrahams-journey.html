The Patriarchs

Abraham's Journey

Genesis 11:31–13:18

; 22

Abram's migration from Ur of the Chaldeans to Haran, then into Canaan, down to Egypt during famine, and back to the hill country where he settled at Hebron.

Ur -> Haran -> Damascus (traditional waypoint) -> Shechem -> Bethel -> The Negev -> Egypt -> Return to the Negev -> Bethel again -> Hebron (Mamre) -> Mount Moriah (traditional Jerusalem)

  • Migration route (approx. 2091 BC, traditional)

Solid segments mark land travel.

About this map

The overall itinerary is given by Genesis; exact roads are not recorded. The route shown follows the caravan corridors of the second millennium BC (historical reconstruction). The Damascus waypoint is a traditional inference from Genesis 15:2.

Description

Genesis traces Abram's movement along the Fertile Crescent: from Ur up the Euphrates valley to Haran, where his father Terah died, then south into Canaan with stops marked by altar-building at Shechem and Bethel. Famine drove him to Egypt, after which he returned to the Negev and Bethel before settling by the oaks of Mamre at Hebron. Years later, God tested Abraham on a mountain in the land of Moriah, traditionally identified with Jerusalem.
