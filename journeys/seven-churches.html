The Early Church

The Seven Churches of Revelation

Revelation 1–3

The seven churches of the province of Asia addressed by the risen Christ through John: Ephesus, Smyrna, Pergamum, Thyatira, Sardis, Philadelphia, and Laodicea.

Patmos -> Ephesus -> Smyrna -> Pergamum -> Thyatira -> Sardis -> Philadelphia -> Laodicea

  • Letter order (approx. AD 95)

Dashed segments mark sea travel or a dashed route circuit.

About this map

All seven sites are securely identified (modern Selçuk, İzmir, Bergama, Akhisar, Sart, Alaşehir, and near Denizli in Türkiye). The connecting line marks the letter order — a plausible Roman-road delivery circuit (scholarly opinion), not a route recorded in the text.

Description

Exiled on Patmos, John was commanded to write what he saw to seven churches of Asia. Their order in Revelation traces a natural courier circuit: north from Ephesus up the coast to Smyrna and Pergamum, then inland and south through Thyatira, Sardis, and Philadelphia to Laodicea in the Lycus valley. Each letter speaks to the city's circumstances — Laodicea's lukewarm water, Sardis' reputation without life, Smyrna's poverty and coming persecution.
