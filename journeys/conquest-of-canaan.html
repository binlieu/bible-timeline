Conquest of Canaan

The Conquest of Canaan

Joshua 1–24

Joshua's campaigns: crossing the Jordan, the fall of Jericho and Ai, the covenant at Shechem, the southern campaign after Gibeon, and the northern campaign against Hazor.

Plains of Moab (Shittim) -> Jordan crossing -> Gilgal -> Jericho -> Ai (et-Tell) -> Mount Ebal (Shechem) -> Gibeon -> Beth-horon -> Azekah -> Lachish -> Hebron -> Waters of Merom -> Hazor -> Shiloh -> Shechem (farewell)

  • Campaign route (approx. 1406–1399 BC, traditional)

Dotted segments mark disputed locations or uncertain segments.

About this map

City identifications follow mainstream historical geography (e.g. Lachish = Tell ed-Duweir, Hazor = Tell el-Qedah); Ai (et-Tell) and the waters of Merom are debated. The date approx. 1406–1375 BC follows the traditional early chronology.

Description

The conquest unfolds in three movements. Israel crossed the Jordan opposite Jericho and established a base at Gilgal; Jericho and Ai fell, and the covenant was renewed at Mount Ebal by Shechem. The Gibeonite treaty then drew Israel into the southern campaign — the long day of battle at Gibeon and the descent of Beth-horon, then the Shephelah strongholds of Azekah, Lachish, and Eglon, and up to Hebron and Debir. Finally the northern coalition under the king of Hazor was defeated by the waters of Merom, and Hazor burned. The land was allotted at Shiloh, and Joshua's farewell covenant was made at Shechem.
