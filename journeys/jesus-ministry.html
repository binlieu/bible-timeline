Life of Jesus

Jesus' Ministry

Matthew; Mark; Luke; John

Key sites of Jesus' life and ministry: from Bethlehem and Nazareth through the Galilee ministry centered on Capernaum, to Jerusalem, the cross, and the resurrection.

Bethlehem -> Nazareth -> Baptism site (al-Maghtas) -> Judean wilderness -> Cana -> Capernaum -> Sychar (Jacob's well) -> Nain -> Bethsaida -> Caesarea Philippi -> Mount Tabor -> Bethany -> Jericho -> Jerusalem -> Mount of Olives -> Emmaus

  • Principal sites (approx. AD 26–30/33)

Dotted segments mark disputed locations or uncertain segments.

About this map

Sites marked 'traditional' rest on early church identification (e.g. the baptism site at al-Maghtas, Mount Tabor for the transfiguration — some scholars prefer Mount Hermon's slopes; Emmaus' location is debated). Dates approx. AD 26–30/33.

Description

The Gospels are not a travel diary — Jesus moved repeatedly between Galilee and Jerusalem for the feasts (John records several such journeys). The map therefore marks the principal sites rather than a single route: the birth at Bethlehem, boyhood at Nazareth, baptism at the Jordan, the Galilean ministry based in Capernaum with works at Cana, Nain, and Bethsaida, the withdrawal north to Caesarea Philippi and the transfiguration, the raising of Lazarus at Bethany, and the final week in Jerusalem.
