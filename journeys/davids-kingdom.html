David's Kingdom

2 Samuel 5; 8; 1 Kings 4:20–21

The united kingdom under David: Israel and Judah with subdued neighbors — Philistia checked, Moab, Edom, Ammon, and Aram-Damascus made tributary.

Jerusalem -> Hebron -> Dan -> Beersheba -> Damascus -> Rabbah

Shaded areas mark approximate regional extent.

About this map

The shaded area is an approximate historical reconstruction of the core kingdom with directly subdued territories (approx. 1010–970 BC); ancient borders were zones of control, not lines. The wider sphere of tribute toward the Euphrates is described in the text but not shaded. Archaeological evidence: the Tel Dan stele's 'House of David' attests the dynasty within about 150 years of David's reign.

Description

David reigned seven years at Hebron and thirty-three at Jerusalem, which he took from the Jebusites and made his capital. 2 Samuel 8 summarizes the wars that secured the kingdom: the Philistines subdued, Moab made tributary, Edom garrisoned, Ammon defeated at Rabbah, and Aram-Damascus brought under tribute after the defeat of Hadadezer of Zobah. Under Solomon this dominion was described as stretching from the Euphrates to the border of Egypt (1 Kings 4:21).
