Paul's Missionary Journeys

Acts 13–28

Paul's three missionary journeys from Antioch — Cyprus and Galatia; Macedonia and Achaia; Ephesus and the Aegean — and his final voyage under guard to Rome.

About this map

City locations are well established; sea lanes and roads (e.g. the Via Egnatia) are drawn schematically between ports and cities. Dates are approximate, anchored on the Gallio inscription at Delphi (Acts 18:12, approx. AD 51–52).

Description

From the church at Antioch Paul was sent out three times. The first journey (with Barnabas, approx. AD 47–48) evangelized Cyprus and the cities of southern Galatia. The second (with Silas, approx. AD 49–52) revisited Galatia, then crossed to Macedonia — Philippi, Thessalonica, Berea — and on to Athens and eighteen months at Corinth. The third (approx. AD 53–57) centered on three years at Ephesus before a farewell tour through Macedonia and Greece and the voyage to Jerusalem. Arrested there, Paul appealed to Caesar and sailed for Rome (approx. AD 59–60), shipwrecked on Malta on the way.
