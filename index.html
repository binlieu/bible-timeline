Primeval History

Creation

approx. symbolic beginning

Genesis 1:1-2:3

God creates the heavens and the earth, forming an ordered world and making humanity in his image. The seventh day is blessed as the pattern of divine rest.

Primeval History

The Fall

approx. symbolic early human history

Genesis 3

Adam and Eve disobey God in Eden and are exiled from the garden. The chapter explains the entrance of sin, shame, death, and hope into the human story.

Primeval History

Cain and Abel

approx. symbolic early human history

Genesis 4:1-16

Cain murders his brother Abel after God regards Abel and his offering. God judges Cain, yet also marks him so vengeance will not consume him.

Primeval History

Godly Line of Seth

approx. symbolic early human history

Genesis 4:25-5:32

Genesis traces the line from Seth to Noah, preserving the family through which the later flood narrative will move. The genealogy emphasizes both human mortality and God’s continuing purposes.

Primeval History

The Flood

approx. symbolic primeval period

Genesis 6-8

God judges the earth’s violence and corruption with a flood while preserving Noah, his family, and living creatures in the ark. The ark comes to rest in the mountains of Ararat as the waters recede.

Primeval History

Noahic Covenant

approx. symbolic post-flood period

Genesis 9:1-17

God blesses Noah’s family, renews the command to fill the earth, and establishes a covenant with all flesh. The rainbow is given as the sign that God will not again destroy all flesh by flood.

Primeval History

Tower of Babel

approx. symbolic primeval period

Genesis 11:1-9

Humanity gathers in Shinar to build a city and tower in order to make a name for itself. God confuses their language and scatters them over the earth.

The Patriarchs

Call of Abraham

approx. 2091 BC

Genesis 12:1-9

God calls Abram to leave his land and promises him land, descendants, blessing, and worldwide blessing through his family. Abram travels through Canaan and builds altars at Shechem and near Bethel.

People

God

God creates, judges, redeems, and keeps covenant throughout the biblical story.

Adam

Adam is the first man, created in God’s image and placed in Eden.

Eve

Eve is the first woman and mother of all living.

Cain

Cain is Adam and Eve’s son who murdered Abel and was sent away from the Lord’s presence.

Abel

Abel is Adam and Eve’s son whose offering was accepted and whose blood cried out after Cain killed him.

Seth

Seth is the son given to Adam and Eve after Abel’s death and part of the line leading to Noah.

Enoch

Enoch walked with God and was taken by God according to Genesis 5.

Methuselah

Methuselah is remembered in Genesis 5 as the longest-lived man in the genealogy from Adam to Noah.

Noah

Noah obeyed God, built the ark, and became the covenant head of humanity after the flood.

Shem

Shem is one of Noah’s sons and ancestor of the line that leads toward Abraham.

Ham

Ham is one of Noah’s sons and is named among those preserved through the flood.

Japheth

Japheth is one of Noah’s sons and is named among the families preserved through the flood.

Abraham

Abraham receives God’s covenant promises of land, offspring, and blessing to the nations.

Sarah

Sarah is Abraham’s wife and Isaac’s mother, through whom God preserved the promised line.

Hagar

Hagar is Sarah’s Egyptian servant and Ishmael’s mother, seen by God in the wilderness.

Ishmael

Ishmael is Abraham and Hagar’s son, preserved by God and promised a great nation.

Lot

Lot is Abraham’s nephew who settled near Sodom and was rescued before its destruction.

Melchizedek

Melchizedek is king of Salem and priest of God Most High who blessed Abram.

Isaac

Isaac is the promised son of Abraham and Sarah through whom the covenant line continues.

Rebekah

Rebekah becomes Isaac’s wife and mother of Jacob and Esau.

Jacob

Jacob, later named Israel, fathers the family that becomes the twelve tribes.

Esau

Esau is Jacob’s twin brother who sold his birthright and lost Isaac’s blessing.

Leah

Leah is Jacob’s wife and mother of several sons, including Judah.

Rachel

Rachel is Jacob’s beloved wife and mother of Joseph and Benjamin.

Joseph

Joseph is sold into Egypt and later preserves his family during famine.

Judah

Judah is Jacob and Leah’s son whose tribe later carries royal significance.

Benjamin

Benjamin is Jacob and Rachel’s youngest son and part of the family that went to Egypt.

Potiphar

Potiphar is the Egyptian official who bought Joseph as a slave.

Moses

Moses leads Israel out of Egypt, receives the law, and shepherds the people through the wilderness.

Aaron

Aaron is Moses’ brother, Israel’s first high priest, and Moses’ spokesman before Pharaoh.

Miriam

Miriam is Moses and Aaron’s sister, a prophet who led worship after the sea crossing.

Jethro

Jethro is Moses’ father-in-law and priest of Midian who counseled Moses.

Joshua

Joshua succeeds Moses and leads Israel into Canaan.

Caleb

Caleb is the faithful spy who trusted God and later received Hebron.

Rahab

Rahab protected Israel’s spies in Jericho and was spared when the city fell.

Balaam

Balaam was hired to curse Israel but spoke blessings under God’s constraint.

Deborah

Deborah judged Israel and summoned Barak in the conflict with Sisera.

Barak

Barak led Israel’s forces at Deborah’s command against Sisera.

Gideon

Gideon was called by God to deliver Israel from Midian.

Jephthah

Jephthah led Gilead against the Ammonites and is remembered for a tragic vow.

Samson

Samson was a Nazirite judge whose strength was used against the Philistines.

Delilah

Delilah betrayed Samson to the Philistines after discovering the secret of his strength.

Ruth

Ruth is a Moabite woman whose loyalty to Naomi led her to Bethlehem and Boaz.

Naomi

Naomi is Ruth’s mother-in-law who returned from Moab to Bethlehem.

Boaz

Boaz redeemed Naomi’s family line and married Ruth.

Eli

Eli was priest and judge at Shiloh when Samuel was called.

Hannah

Hannah prayed for a son and dedicated Samuel to the Lord.

Samuel

Samuel was called by God as a prophet and judged Israel at the transition toward monarchy.

Saul

Saul was Israel's first king, anointed by Samuel and later rejected for disobedience.

Jonathan

Jonathan was Saul's son and David's covenant friend.

David

David was the shepherd from Bethlehem who became king over all Israel and received the Davidic covenant.

Goliath

Goliath was the Philistine champion defeated by David in the Valley of Elah.

Bathsheba

Bathsheba was the wife of David and mother of Solomon.

Nathan

Nathan was the prophet who delivered the Davidic covenant promise and confronted David's sin.

Absalom

Absalom was David's son who led a rebellion against his father.

Joab

Joab was David's powerful army commander.

Solomon

Solomon was David's son, known for wisdom, wealth, and building the temple.

Rehoboam

Rehoboam was Solomon's son whose harsh rule helped divide the kingdom.

Jeroboam

Jeroboam became the first king of the northern kingdom of Israel.

Ahab

Ahab was a northern king whose reign was marked by Baal worship and conflict with Elijah.

Jezebel

Jezebel was Ahab's queen and a major promoter of Baal worship in Israel.

Elijah

Elijah was a prophet who confronted Ahab, Jezebel, and Baal worship in Israel.

Elisha

Elisha succeeded Elijah and ministered in the northern kingdom through signs and prophetic counsel.

Naaman

Naaman was an Aramean commander healed through Elisha's prophetic word.

Jonah

Jonah was the prophet sent to warn Nineveh.

Amos

Amos warned Israel about injustice, false worship, and coming judgment.

Hosea

Hosea warned the northern kingdom through a message of covenant betrayal and restoring mercy.

Isaiah

Hezekiah

Sennacherib

Josiah

Jeremiah

Jeremiah warned Judah before Jerusalem fell and promised a new covenant.

Meshach was one of Daniel's faithful friends in Babylon.

Mordecai

Haggai

Zechariah

Zechariah encouraged the restored community with visions of renewal and future hope.
